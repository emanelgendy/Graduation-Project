muscle group best exercise steps Picture for exercise
Chest Pushups Start in a high plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart.
Lower your chest towards the ground by bending your elbows.
Push back up to the starting position.
Chest Bench Press Lie on a flat bench holding a barbell with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width.
Lower the bar to your chest, then press it back up.
Back Lat Pulldowns Sit at a lat pulldown machine and grab the bar with an overhand, shoulder-width grip.
Pull the bar down towards your upper chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
Slowly return the bar to the starting position.
Back Bent-Over Rows Stand with your knees slightly bent, hinge forward at the hips, and let your arms hang straight down.
Keeping your back flat, row the weight up towards your lower chest, squeezing your shoulder blades.
Slowly lower the weight back down.
Shoulders Overhead Press Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a barbell at shoulder height with an overhand grip.
Press the bar straight overhead, locking your arms out.
Slowly lower the bar back to the starting position.
Shoulders Lateral Raises Stand with a dumbbell in each hand at your sides, palms facing inwards.
Raise the weights out to the sides until your arms are parallel to the ground.
Slowly lower the weights back down.
Arms Bicep Curls Stand holding a dumbbell in each hand with an underhand grip, arms extended at your sides.
Bend at the elbows to curl the weights up towards your shoulders.
Slowly lower the weights back down.
Arms Tricep Pushdowns Stand in front of a cable machine, grab the straight bar attachment with an overhand grip.
Push the weight down by extending your arms, keeping your upper arms stationary.
Slowly return to the starting position.
Legs Squats Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a barbell across the back of your shoulders.
Sit your hips back and bend your knees to lower into a squat.
Drive through your heels to stand back up.
Legs Deadlifts Stand with your feet hip-width apart, hinge at the hips to grip a barbell on the floor.
Keeping your back flat, drive through your heels to stand up straight, lifting the bar.
Slowly lower the bar back to the floor.
